Patio Covers and Pergolas / Arbors

You’re planning to add a beautiful Pergola or Arbor to your home or even a Landscape design, it’s an exciting project. Enhancing your home and your garden with patios and pergolas is an excellent way of improving your home and the quality of your home life. Build either in your back garden and you immediately have an outdoor living space, and as long as you use quality materials… There couldn’t be an easier or quicker way to add value and improve the aspect of your home. Why not make more use of the quiet outdoors, take in some fresh air, read a book and enjoy the sunset by enhancing your garden and creating shady outdoor living spaces.

Patios change the look and ambience of your home, but when you add a pergola covering, it gives your outside space a much cosier and intimate feel. A pergola at the end of your patio makes a great breakfast nook for the family to enjoy.

Benefits of a Pergola or Arbor Often pergolas are built of existing structures such as the house. This can be a great way to provide a little bit of shading for decks and patios that are adjacent to the home. In this case they can perform much like awnings do, and can even help keep your air conditioning bills down by intercepting sunlight that otherwise would be warming the house. Shading is important not only for the direct light from the sun, but also from reflected light. Anyone who’s ever stood on a light concrete patio during the summer can attest to how much heat and light can be radiated from it. By shading the patio itself, you are keeping that radiation from at bay as well.

Benefits of cedar wood Pergola or Arbor Cedar wood has remarkable dimensional stability. This means the wood doesn’t change its size or dimension despite weather, humidity or temperature conditions. Many types of wood will warp when subjected to moisture, but cedar stays straight and flat which makes it perfect for Pergolas or arbors.
